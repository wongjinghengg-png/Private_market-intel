The week in brief
The defining throughline this week is the accelerating institutionalization of defense-adjacent and AI-infrastructure capital, with private markets doing the heavy lifting that public investors are increasingly reluctant to underwrite — Oracle's worst week since the dot-com bust signals that public markets are growing skeptical of open-ended AI capex promises, while private transactions from Quantum Systems' $1.2 billion raise to Lockheed's pursuit of Ultra Maritime and Anduril's Air Force contracts show that defense-tech and dual-use infrastructure are attracting conviction capital outside the scrutiny of quarterly earnings calls. ByteDance's twin moves — a $20 billion offshore loan and a $38 billion Brazil data center — underscore that the largest AI infrastructure bets are being structured privately and cross-border, partly to sidestep the governance pressures that hammer public valuations. The one thing to watch is whether the Oracle selloff marks a genuine inflection in public-market tolerance for unanchored AI spend, which would widen the valuation gap between private defense-and-infrastructure deals and their listed peers and reshape how sponsors think about exit timing.
