The week in brief
The defining throughline this week is the collision of defense-tech capital formation with AI infrastructure anxiety: private markets are flooding into autonomous and maritime defense platforms — from Advent's $3.5 billion Ultra Maritime sale to Lockheed, to Quantum Systems' $1.2 billion raise, to Anduril and General Atomics winning Air Force drone contracts — precisely as public markets signal unease about whether AI's capital requirements can generate returns, with Oracle posting its worst week since the dot-com bust and ByteDance simultaneously tapping $20 billion in offshore loans while breaking ground on a $38 billion Brazil data center. The private-public tension is acute: sponsors and growth investors are rotating toward defense tech as a cleaner AI monetization story, while listed hyperscaler proxies absorb the uncertainty of when and whether civilian AI spend clears the return hurdle. The one thing to watch is whether ByteDance's record offshore loan prices cleanly — its execution will be the clearest real-time stress test of institutional appetite for large-scale AI infrastructure debt in a risk-off public equity environment.
